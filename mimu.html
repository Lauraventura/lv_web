Mimu is a smart diary meant to recognize both toxic and healthy patterns of behaviour. It is a tool to encourage positive routines and collect good memories that will help you through difficult times.

If you wish to know about the origin and evolution of the project, keep scrolling.

↡

Research

Given the subject I was dealing with, I focused my research on the interviews. Nevertheless, I still used surveys to check some facts so that my own opinion would not alter the results.

The conclusions confirmed something crucial: there is certain anxiety triggered by the fact that people suffering from cyclical depression don’t always pick the signs when they are about to relapse. That feeling is quite permanent, and it is usually difficult to tell if it is based on facts or just fear of relapse.

↡

Persona

My persona was clearly someone with depression, but also a person who needs a simple system to keep track of her emotions (on her own or for therapy). This is important, since people with depression often have trouble maintaining a routine and finding the strength to perform simple tasks.

She also is afraid that talking or seeing bad things that happened to her will affect her very negatively.

↡

MVP

I knew this product should never intend to replace a doctor, but needed to be useful to people in some other way. A typical struggle was traking things that happened to them. This is useful for two reasons: learning from the past, and letting the good memories assist you when you are in a bad place. My main features were two dashboards: one with statistics about every memorie the user introduced, and one with just the good memories, intended to be a safe place to remember that despite everything, there are good moments too.

↡

Information architecture

There were so many layers of information to this project, that testing was fundamental to the building of the project. I had to change a couple times the whole structure of the app to make it simpler to navigate. Changes are a tough decision to make, but necessary in early stages of a project to avoid further problems. This project was a big learning in that sense.

↡

UI

I needed to set two moods, visually, as well: one for the analytic data, and one for the joyful data. That’s why I made two different color palettes, that I tried to unite with a similar use of typography. This was the most challenging part of the whole project (which was very challenging every step of the way), as I sort of needed to to create two dialects of the same language. But there was another challenge: how to translate all the data into graphic information while keeping it positive.

↡

Prototype

Here you have the link to the prototype of the project. Of course, it is not a full working prototype, and it is a simulation of a data view in the app. Nevertheless, when tested, this prototype allowed me to check my users connection to the product, and I felt it honoured all the previous research and testing. Of course, further testing will better the final product, but this materialization of the concept engaged users and validated the whole process.
