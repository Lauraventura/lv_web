Mimu is a smart diary meant to recognize both toxic and healthy patterns of behaviour. It is a tool to encourage positive routines and collect good memories that will help you through difficult times.

If you wish to know about the origin and evolution of the project, keep scrolling.

↡

Research

Given the subject I was dealing with, I focused my research on the interviews. Nevertheless, I still used surveys to check some facts so that my own opinion would not alter the results.

The conclusions confirmed something crucial: there is certain anxiety triggered by the fact that people suffering from cyclical depression don’t always pick the signs when they are about to relapse. That feeling is quite permanent, and it is usually difficult to tell if it is based on facts or just fear of relapse.

↡

Concept

I had to create a product to help people with depression to cope with the ups and downs affecting them. But it was complex: as research showed, they are sometimes in need of affection and hope, while in some other occasions they require facts and data to connect them to reality.

I came up with the idea to track the user’s feelings in order to offer two main features: a dashboard with statistics, and a feed with all the good memories the user has registered. Based on those memories, the product also suggests creating alarms to help achieve healthy routines.

↡

IxD

Now that the main features were established, I could create the first prototype of the app. It allowed me to test the flows and create smoother navigation, but it also was the first chance I had to show users a materialized version of my idea.

I think at this point one can see if you are honoring the research you made or not: if it is, users will be engaged, they will feel connected to the project and will give you meaningful feedback. I feel like this was the last check I needed to validate the whole concept.

↡

UI

I wanted the visuals to stay connected to the needs of my persona, to keep in mind all the previous research. Considering I had two main features that related to different feelings and required distinct content, I decided to create two color palettes, that would help create that “switch” from one state to the other.

A palette of blue would be the one for analytic data, statistics, facts. Then a pink palette for the emotional content, the one that gives hope and ignores all bad things.
